STATINS AND CHOLESTEROL MEDICATIONS — Reference Guide

OVERVIEW:
Statins are the most commonly prescribed medications for lowering cholesterol. They reduce LDL ("bad") cholesterol and cardiovascular risk. Other cholesterol-lowering medications are available for patients who cannot tolerate statins or need additional lowering.

STATIN MEDICATIONS:

High-Intensity Statins (lower LDL by 50% or more):
- Atorvastatin (Lipitor) 40-80 mg
- Rosuvastatin (Crestor) 20-40 mg

Moderate-Intensity Statins (lower LDL by 30-50%):
- Atorvastatin 10-20 mg
- Rosuvastatin 5-10 mg
- Simvastatin (Zocor) 20-40 mg
- Pravastatin (Pravachol) 40-80 mg

Low-Intensity Statins (lower LDL by less than 30%):
- Simvastatin 10 mg
- Pravastatin 10-20 mg
- Fluvastatin 20-40 mg

HOW STATINS WORK:
- Block HMG-CoA reductase enzyme in the liver
- Liver produces less cholesterol
- Liver removes more LDL from blood
- Also stabilize arterial plaques and reduce inflammation

COMMON SIDE EFFECTS:
- Muscle pain/weakness (myalgia) — most common reason for stopping
- Elevated liver enzymes (usually mild and reversible)
- Digestive problems (nausea, diarrhea, constipation)
- Headache
- Slightly increased blood sugar (small increase in diabetes risk)

SERIOUS BUT RARE SIDE EFFECTS:
- Rhabdomyolysis (severe muscle breakdown — very rare)
- Liver damage (rare — monitor liver enzymes)
- Memory issues (reported but not conclusively proven)

OTHER CHOLESTEROL MEDICATIONS:

1. Ezetimibe (Zetia):
   - How it works: Blocks cholesterol absorption in intestine
   - Lowers LDL by 15-20% (additional when added to statin)
   - Well tolerated, few side effects
   - Often combined with statin for additional lowering

2. PCSK9 Inhibitors:
   - Evolocumab (Repatha), Alirocumab (Praluent)
   - Injectable (every 2-4 weeks)
   - Lowers LDL by 50-60%
   - Used for familial hypercholesterolemia or very high risk patients
   - Expensive

3. Fibrates:
   - Fenofibrate (Tricor), Gemfibrozil (Lopid)
   - Primarily lower triglycerides (by 25-50%)
   - Modest HDL increase
   - Caution: Gemfibrozil + statin increases muscle damage risk

4. Omega-3 Fatty Acids (Prescription):
   - Icosapent ethyl (Vascepa)
   - Lowers triglycerides
   - Shown to reduce cardiovascular events when added to statin

5. Bile Acid Sequestrants:
   - Cholestyramine, Colesevelam
   - Bind bile acids, forcing liver to use cholesterol to make more
   - Lower LDL by 15-25%
   - Side effects: GI complaints, constipation

MONITORING ON STATIN THERAPY:
- Lipid panel: 4-12 weeks after starting, then annually
- Liver enzymes: At baseline, then as needed
- Report any muscle pain or weakness to your doctor
- HbA1c if risk factors for diabetes

IMPORTANT NOTES:
- Statins are among the most evidence-based medications in medicine
- Benefits far outweigh risks for most patients
- Take statins at the same time daily (some are best taken at night)
- Grapefruit can interact with some statins (atorvastatin, simvastatin)
- Muscle symptoms often improve with switching to a different statin
- Coenzyme Q10 supplements may help with muscle symptoms (evidence mixed)
