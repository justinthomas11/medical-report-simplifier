COMMON ANTIBIOTICS — Reference Guide

OVERVIEW:
Antibiotics are medications used to treat bacterial infections. They do NOT work against viral infections (like colds, flu, or COVID-19). Proper use of antibiotics is important to prevent antibiotic resistance.

MAJOR ANTIBIOTIC CLASSES:

1. Penicillins:
   - Examples: Amoxicillin, Ampicillin, Amoxicillin-Clavulanate (Augmentin)
   - Used for: Ear infections, sinusitis, strep throat, UTIs, pneumonia, dental infections
   - Side effects: Diarrhea, nausea, rash
   - Allergy: ~10% of people report penicillin allergy (most can tolerate it after testing)

2. Cephalosporins:
   - Examples: Cephalexin (Keflex), Cefuroxime, Ceftriaxone, Cefdinir
   - Used for: Skin infections, UTIs, pneumonia, surgical prophylaxis
   - Generations: 1st through 5th (broader coverage with higher generations)
   - Cross-allergy with penicillin: ~1-2% (low risk)

3. Macrolides:
   - Examples: Azithromycin (Z-pack), Clarithromycin, Erythromycin
   - Used for: Respiratory infections, skin infections, STIs, H. pylori
   - Side effects: GI upset, QT prolongation (heart rhythm concern)
   - Azithromycin: Often prescribed as 5-day course

4. Fluoroquinolones:
   - Examples: Ciprofloxacin (Cipro), Levofloxacin (Levaquin), Moxifloxacin
   - Used for: UTIs, respiratory infections, GI infections
   - Side effects: Tendon damage, nerve damage, QT prolongation
   - FDA black box warning: Reserved for when no other options available

5. Tetracyclines:
   - Examples: Doxycycline, Minocycline, Tetracycline
   - Used for: Acne, respiratory infections, Lyme disease, malaria prevention
   - Side effects: Sun sensitivity, GI upset, tooth discoloration in children
   - Take with food; avoid dairy products near dosing time

6. Sulfonamides:
   - Examples: Trimethoprim-Sulfamethoxazole (Bactrim/Septra)
   - Used for: UTIs, ear infections, traveler's diarrhea, MRSA skin infections
   - Side effects: Rash, sun sensitivity, blood count changes
   - Allergy: Sulfa allergy is common

7. Metronidazole (Flagyl):
   - Used for: Anaerobic bacterial infections, C. difficile, dental infections, parasites
   - Side effects: Metallic taste, nausea
   - Important: Do NOT drink alcohol while taking (causes severe nausea/vomiting)

8. Nitrofurantoin (Macrobid):
   - Used for: UTIs only (does not reach adequate levels elsewhere)
   - Side effects: GI upset, lung problems with long-term use
   - Avoid in kidney disease

IMPORTANT CONCEPTS:
- Complete the full course even if you feel better
- Antibiotic resistance is a growing global threat
- Don't take antibiotics for viral infections
- Culture and sensitivity (C&S) tests identify which antibiotic works best
- Probiotics may help prevent antibiotic-associated diarrhea
- Some antibiotics interact with other medications (especially warfarin, birth control)

WHEN TO SEE A DOCTOR:
- Allergic reactions (rash, swelling, difficulty breathing) — seek immediate care
- Severe diarrhea (may indicate C. difficile infection)
- No improvement after 48-72 hours of treatment
- Worsening symptoms while on antibiotics
