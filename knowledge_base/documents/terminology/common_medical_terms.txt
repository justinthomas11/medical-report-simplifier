COMMON MEDICAL TERMS EXPLAINED — Reference Guide

This glossary explains medical terms commonly found in medical reports in simple language.

GENERAL TERMS:
- Acute: Sudden onset, short duration (e.g., acute infection)
- Chronic: Long-lasting, ongoing (e.g., chronic kidney disease)
- Benign: Not cancerous, not harmful
- Malignant: Cancerous, capable of spreading
- Bilateral: Affecting both sides
- Unilateral: Affecting one side
- Asymptomatic: Without symptoms
- Symptomatic: Having symptoms
- Idiopathic: Of unknown cause
- Etiology: The cause of a disease
- Prognosis: Expected outcome or course of a disease
- Comorbidity: Having two or more conditions at the same time
- Contraindicated: Should not be used or done (potentially harmful)
- Prophylactic: Preventive treatment

CONDITION DESCRIPTORS:
- -itis: Inflammation (e.g., arthritis = joint inflammation, hepatitis = liver inflammation)
- -osis: Abnormal condition (e.g., fibrosis = scarring, stenosis = narrowing)
- -emia: Blood condition (e.g., anemia = low red blood cells, hyperglycemia = high blood sugar)
- -penia: Deficiency (e.g., thrombocytopenia = low platelets, leukopenia = low white blood cells)
- -megaly: Enlargement (e.g., hepatomegaly = enlarged liver, splenomegaly = enlarged spleen)
- -ectomy: Surgical removal (e.g., appendectomy = removal of appendix)
- -otomy: Cutting into (e.g., tracheotomy = cutting into the windpipe)
- -scopy: Looking inside (e.g., endoscopy = looking inside the digestive tract)
- -graphy: Recording/imaging (e.g., radiography = X-ray imaging)

BODY SYSTEM PREFIXES:
- Cardio-: Heart (cardiovascular = heart and blood vessels)
- Hepato-: Liver (hepatitis = liver inflammation)
- Nephro-/Renal: Kidney (nephropathy = kidney disease)
- Pulmo-: Lung (pulmonary = related to lungs)
- Neuro-: Nerve/brain (neuropathy = nerve damage)
- Gastro-: Stomach (gastritis = stomach inflammation)
- Hemo-/Hemato-: Blood (hematology = study of blood)
- Osteo-: Bone (osteoporosis = weak/brittle bones)
- Derm-: Skin (dermatitis = skin inflammation)
- Endo-: Within/internal (endocrine = hormonal system)

COMMON REPORT TERMS:
- Within normal limits (WNL): Results are in the expected range
- Unremarkable: Nothing unusual or abnormal found
- Indicated: Recommended or suggested
- Elevated: Higher than normal
- Depressed/Decreased: Lower than normal
- Borderline: At the edge of normal/abnormal
- Significant: Important, meaningful (medically relevant)
- Incidental finding: Something found unexpectedly during testing for something else
- Differential diagnosis: List of possible conditions that could explain symptoms
- Follow-up: Return visit or additional testing needed
- Impression: Doctor's interpretation or conclusion
- Findings: What was observed or measured

SEVERITY TERMS:
- Mild: Slight, minor
- Moderate: Medium severity
- Severe: Serious, significant
- Critical: Life-threatening, requires immediate attention
- Stable: Not changing, consistent
- Progressive: Getting worse over time
- Resolving: Getting better
- Refractory: Not responding to treatment
