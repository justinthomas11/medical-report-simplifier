LABORATORY UNITS REFERENCE — Guide

COMMON LAB UNITS AND WHAT THEY MEAN:

1. mg/dL (milligrams per deciliter):
   - Used for: glucose, cholesterol, creatinine, bilirubin, BUN, calcium, uric acid
   - Measures: Weight of substance per volume of blood
   - Conversion: 1 mg/dL = 0.01 g/L

2. mmol/L (millimoles per liter):
   - Used for: glucose (international), electrolytes (sodium, potassium, chloride)
   - SI unit equivalent of mg/dL for many tests
   - Glucose conversion: mg/dL ÷ 18 = mmol/L

3. g/dL (grams per deciliter):
   - Used for: hemoglobin, total protein, albumin
   - Larger unit than mg/dL

4. U/L or IU/L (units per liter):
   - Used for: liver enzymes (ALT, AST, ALP, GGT), lipase, amylase
   - Measures enzyme activity

5. mEq/L (milliequivalents per liter):
   - Used for: electrolytes (sodium, potassium, chloride, bicarbonate)
   - Measures electrical charge of ions

6. cells/mcL or cells/μL (cells per microliter):
   - Used for: WBC count, RBC count, platelet count
   - May also be expressed as x10^3/μL or x10^6/μL

7. fL (femtoliters):
   - Used for: MCV (mean corpuscular volume), MPV (mean platelet volume)
   - Measures cell size (1 fL = 10^-15 liters)

8. pg (picograms):
   - Used for: MCH (mean corpuscular hemoglobin)
   - Measures weight at cellular level

9. % (percent):
   - Used for: hematocrit, HbA1c, reticulocytes, differential WBC count
   - Proportion of total

10. mm/hr (millimeters per hour):
    - Used for: ESR (erythrocyte sedimentation rate)
    - Measures rate of red blood cell settling

11. ng/mL (nanograms per milliliter):
    - Used for: vitamin D, vitamin B12, ferritin, PSA, troponin
    - Very small concentration unit

12. mIU/L or μIU/mL (milli-international units per liter):
    - Used for: TSH, insulin
    - Measures hormone activity

13. pg/mL (picograms per milliliter):
    - Used for: Free T3, BNP, vitamin B12
    - Extremely small concentration

14. ng/dL (nanograms per deciliter):
    - Used for: Free T4, testosterone
    - Small hormone concentrations

15. mmHg (millimeters of mercury):
    - Used for: blood pressure, partial pressure of gases
    - Systolic/diastolic (e.g., 120/80 mmHg)

16. bpm (beats per minute):
    - Used for: heart rate
    - Normal resting: 60-100 bpm

17. mL/min/1.73m2:
    - Used for: eGFR (estimated glomerular filtration rate)
    - Measures kidney filtration rate normalized to body surface area

UNDERSTANDING "NORMAL RANGES":
- Normal ranges vary by laboratory, age, sex, and ethnicity
- Reference ranges are typically set to include 95% of healthy individuals
- Values slightly outside the range are not always concerning
- Trends over time are often more important than a single value
- Always interpret results in clinical context
- Lab reports typically flag values as "H" (high) or "L" (low)
