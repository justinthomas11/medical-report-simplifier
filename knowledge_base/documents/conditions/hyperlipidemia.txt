HYPERLIPIDEMIA (HIGH CHOLESTEROL) — Reference Guide

OVERVIEW:
Hyperlipidemia refers to elevated levels of lipids (fats) in the blood, including cholesterol and triglycerides. It is a major risk factor for cardiovascular disease, including heart attack and stroke. It usually has no symptoms and is detected through blood tests.

TYPES:
1. Hypercholesterolemia: Elevated total and/or LDL cholesterol
2. Hypertriglyceridemia: Elevated triglycerides
3. Mixed hyperlipidemia: Both cholesterol and triglycerides elevated
4. Familial hypercholesterolemia: Genetic condition causing very high LDL from birth

RISK ASSESSMENT:
- 10-year cardiovascular risk is calculated using factors: age, sex, race, cholesterol, blood pressure, diabetes, smoking
- Low risk: <5% — lifestyle changes
- Borderline: 5-7.5% — consider medication if risk enhancers present
- Intermediate: 7.5-20% — moderate-intensity statin recommended
- High risk: >20% — high-intensity statin recommended

TREATMENT TARGETS:
- General population: LDL <100 mg/dL
- High cardiovascular risk: LDL <70 mg/dL
- Very high risk (prior heart attack/stroke): LDL <55 mg/dL
- Triglycerides: <150 mg/dL
- HDL: >40 mg/dL (men), >50 mg/dL (women)

LIFESTYLE MODIFICATIONS:
- Heart-healthy diet (Mediterranean, DASH): reduce saturated fat to <7% of calories, eliminate trans fats
- Regular exercise: 150 minutes moderate or 75 minutes vigorous per week
- Weight management: Lose 5-10% body weight if overweight
- Smoking cessation
- Limit alcohol intake
- Increase fiber intake (25-30g/day)
- Plant sterols and stanols (2g/day can lower LDL by 5-15%)

MEDICATIONS:
1. Statins (first-line): Atorvastatin, Rosuvastatin, Simvastatin — lower LDL by 30-50%
2. Ezetimibe: Blocks cholesterol absorption — lowers LDL by additional 15-20%
3. PCSK9 inhibitors: Injectable — lowers LDL by 50-60% (for very high risk)
4. Fibrates: Primarily lower triglycerides
5. Omega-3 fatty acids: Lower triglycerides
6. Bile acid sequestrants: Lower LDL

IMPORTANT NOTES:
- Hyperlipidemia has NO symptoms — screening is essential
- Statins are among the most studied and effective cardiovascular medications
- Lifestyle changes are always the foundation, even with medication
- Regular lipid panel monitoring (every 4-12 weeks after starting medication, then annually)
- Some statins can affect liver enzymes — monitoring may be needed
