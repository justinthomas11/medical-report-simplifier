THYROID FUNCTION TESTS — Reference Guide

OVERVIEW:
Thyroid function tests measure how well the thyroid gland is working. The thyroid is a butterfly-shaped gland in the neck that produces hormones controlling metabolism, energy, heart rate, body temperature, and growth.

COMPONENTS AND NORMAL RANGES:

1. Thyroid-Stimulating Hormone (TSH):
   - Normal: 0.4-4.0 mIU/L
   - High: Hypothyroidism (underactive thyroid) — the pituitary produces more TSH to stimulate a sluggish thyroid
   - Low: Hyperthyroidism (overactive thyroid) — the pituitary reduces TSH because the thyroid is already overproducing
   - Most important screening test for thyroid disorders
   - TSH is produced by the pituitary gland, NOT the thyroid

2. Free T4 (Free Thyroxine):
   - Normal: 0.8-1.8 ng/dL
   - High: Hyperthyroidism, excessive thyroid medication
   - Low: Hypothyroidism, pituitary disorders
   - T4 is the main hormone produced by the thyroid
   - "Free" means the unbound, active form

3. Free T3 (Free Triiodothyronine):
   - Normal: 2.3-4.2 pg/mL
   - High: Hyperthyroidism (especially T3 thyrotoxicosis)
   - Low: Severe hypothyroidism, non-thyroidal illness
   - T3 is the more active thyroid hormone
   - Most T3 is converted from T4 in tissues

4. Total T4:
   - Normal: 5.0-12.0 mcg/dL
   - Includes both bound and unbound T4
   - Affected by pregnancy, estrogen, liver disease (which change binding proteins)

5. Total T3:
   - Normal: 80-200 ng/dL
   - Less commonly ordered than Free T3
   - Useful in diagnosing T3 thyrotoxicosis

6. Thyroid Antibodies:
   - TPO Antibodies (anti-thyroid peroxidase): Normal <35 IU/mL
   - Thyroglobulin Antibodies: Normal <20 IU/mL
   - TSI (Thyroid Stimulating Immunoglobulin): Normal <140%
   - Elevated TPO: Hashimoto thyroiditis (most common cause of hypothyroidism)
   - Elevated TSI: Graves disease (most common cause of hyperthyroidism)

HYPOTHYROIDISM (Underactive Thyroid):
- TSH: HIGH, Free T4: LOW
- Symptoms: Fatigue, weight gain, cold intolerance, constipation, dry skin, hair loss, depression, slow heart rate
- Most common cause: Hashimoto thyroiditis (autoimmune)
- Treatment: Levothyroxine (synthetic T4 replacement)

HYPERTHYROIDISM (Overactive Thyroid):
- TSH: LOW, Free T4: HIGH and/or Free T3: HIGH
- Symptoms: Weight loss, rapid heart rate, anxiety, tremors, heat intolerance, sweating, frequent bowel movements, bulging eyes
- Most common cause: Graves disease (autoimmune)
- Treatment: Antithyroid medications, radioactive iodine, surgery

SUBCLINICAL THYROID DISEASE:
- Subclinical hypothyroidism: TSH slightly high, Free T4 normal
- Subclinical hyperthyroidism: TSH slightly low, Free T4 normal
- May or may not require treatment depending on symptoms and degree

IMPORTANT NOTES:
- TSH is the single best screening test for thyroid disease
- TSH and thyroid hormones have an inverse relationship
- Biotin supplements can interfere with thyroid test results
- Thyroid disease is more common in women
- Pregnancy significantly affects thyroid hormone levels
